For interpreters

Claim a job in two taps. See what you'll be paid before you accept.

Phone-first. Quiet by default. Get paid on the day the agency promised, not "around the 15th of next month."

You're not the customer in the contract sense — agencies are. But you're the only reason the contract has any value, and the product treats you that way.

The phone app, in plain terms.

  • Two-tap claim. See the rate, the consumer's name (or initials per agency policy), the venue, the team. Tap "Claim." Tap "Confirm."
  • See your pay before you accept. Hourly, per-event, mileage, premium pay, anything else — itemized.
  • Quiet by default. You choose which job types ping you. Off-hours mode is one toggle.
  • Workload balance is visible. See your own rotation slot in the queue and why a job did or didn't come to you.
  • 1099 year-to-date strip. Open the app, see your earnings in seconds.
  • Get paid on the day the agency promised. Stripe Connect Express by default. Manual ACH fallback for long-tenured folks who prefer it.

Payment cadence

Paid when the agency promised. Tracked when the agency hasn't.

Default: Friday after the job

Most agencies pay on the Friday following the job week. We surface that date in the app the moment you claim.

Instant payouts (opt-in)

Where the agency enables it, you can opt-in per payout to receive funds same-day for a flat fee (Stripe's published rate, passed through).

Aging visible to you

Open invoices the agency hasn't paid show their aging in your dashboard. No more guessing where your money is.

Fairness dashboard.

You see your own data — how often you've been offered jobs, claim rate, fill rate, average rating, rotation position. Per D7 #8 of the PRD, this is yours to read; we don't gate it.
